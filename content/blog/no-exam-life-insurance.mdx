# No Exam Life Insurance: Your Complete Guide

You know you need life insurance, but the thought of scheduling a medical exam — the blood draw, the urine sample, the nurse coming to your house — keeps you putting it off. Maybe you have a health condition that makes you nervous about what the exam will reveal. Maybe your schedule is too packed to coordinate an appointment. Or maybe you just want coverage **now**, not six weeks from now.

Whatever your reason, **no exam life insurance lets you skip the medical examination entirely.** You can apply online, answer a few questions (or none at all), and have coverage in place within days — sometimes hours.

But no exam life insurance isn't one-size-fits-all. There are three distinct types, each with different costs, coverage limits, and qualification standards. Choosing the wrong one could mean paying far more than necessary or getting far less coverage than you need.

This guide breaks down every no exam option so you can make an informed decision.

## Three Types of No Exam Life Insurance

The term "no exam life insurance" is an umbrella that covers three very different products. Understanding the differences is essential.

### 1. Accelerated Underwriting

Accelerated underwriting is the newest and most sophisticated option. Instead of a medical exam, the insurer uses **data-driven methods** to evaluate your health:

- **Prescription drug databases** (MIB, Rx records)
- **Motor vehicle records**
- **Credit-based insurance scores**
- **Electronic health records**
- **Wearable device data** (some carriers)
- **Facial analytics** (emerging technology)

**How it works:** You apply online or with an agent, answer health questions, and authorize the insurer to pull your records electronically. An algorithm evaluates your risk profile in real time. If you qualify, you receive a decision within hours or days — with **no exam and no waiting period.**

**Who qualifies:** Generally healthy individuals ages 18-60. If the algorithm flags a concern, you may be asked to complete a traditional exam.

**Coverage limits:** Up to **$1 million or more**, comparable to traditional policies.

**Cost:** Only about **10% to 20% more** than fully underwritten policies — sometimes the same price if your health profile is strong.

**Best for:** Healthy individuals who want fast approval and high coverage without the inconvenience of an exam.

### 2. Simplified Issue

Simplified issue policies require you to answer a set of **health questions** on the application but skip the medical exam entirely. The questions typically cover:

- Have you been hospitalized in the past 2-5 years?
- Have you been diagnosed with cancer, heart disease, diabetes, or other serious conditions?
- Do you use tobacco?
- Are you currently taking medications for specific conditions?
- Have you been declined for life insurance before?

**How it works:** You answer the questions truthfully. The insurer makes a decision based solely on your answers — no lab work, no doctor's records (in most cases). Approval can come within **24 to 72 hours.**

**Who qualifies:** A broader range of applicants than accelerated underwriting, including people with some health conditions. However, answering "yes" to certain questions can lead to a decline.

**Coverage limits:** Typically **$50,000 to $500,000**, though some carriers offer up to $1 million.

**Cost:** About **20% to 50% more** than fully underwritten policies.

**Best for:** People with minor health issues who want coverage without the delay and hassle of full underwriting, or anyone who needs moderate coverage quickly.

If you have diabetes or another pre-existing condition, simplified issue can be a practical path — our guide on [life insurance for diabetics](/blog/life-insurance-for-diabetics) covers this in more depth.

### 3. Guaranteed Issue

Guaranteed issue is the safety net of life insurance. **No exam. No health questions. Everyone is accepted** regardless of health status.

**How it works:** You fill out a basic application with your name, age, and beneficiary information. No medical questions are asked. If you meet the age requirements (typically 40-85), you're approved.

**Who qualifies:** Everyone within the age range, regardless of health. This is the policy of last resort for people who cannot qualify for any other type of coverage.

**Coverage limits:** Usually **$5,000 to $25,000**, with some carriers offering up to $50,000.

**Cost:** The **most expensive** per dollar of coverage. Premiums can be 3 to 5 times higher than a traditional policy with the same death benefit.

**The catch — the waiting period:** Most guaranteed issue policies include a **2 to 3 year graded death benefit period.** If you die within the first 2-3 years (from natural causes), the insurer does not pay the full death benefit. Instead, they return your premiums paid plus a percentage (typically 10%) as interest. Accidental death is usually covered in full from day one.

**Best for:** People with serious health conditions (active cancer, advanced diabetes, recent heart attack) who have been declined by other carriers and need at least some coverage for final expenses.

## Pros and Cons of No Exam Life Insurance

### Advantages

**Speed.** Traditional underwriting takes 4 to 8 weeks. No exam policies can be issued in hours to days. If you need coverage immediately — you're closing on a house, starting a business, or just don't want to leave your family unprotected while you wait — this speed is invaluable.

**Convenience.** No scheduling an exam appointment, no awkward visit from a paramedical examiner, no fasting for blood work. You apply from your couch on your phone.

**Accessibility.** If you have health conditions that would make traditional underwriting difficult — diabetes, a history of cancer, heart disease, obesity — simplified or guaranteed issue products give you a path to coverage. Not everyone can pass a medical exam, but everyone deserves life insurance.

**Privacy.** Some people simply don't want an insurance company poking through their blood work. Accelerated underwriting still pulls records, but simplified issue is based solely on your answers.

### Disadvantages

**Higher cost.** You're paying a premium for convenience and reduced underwriting. Over a 20 or 30-year term, that extra cost adds up significantly.

**Lower coverage limits.** If you need $1 million or more in coverage, your options narrow to accelerated underwriting. Simplified and guaranteed issue won't get you there.

**Waiting periods (guaranteed issue only).** The graded death benefit means your family isn't fully protected for the first 2-3 years. This defeats the purpose if you need immediate coverage for a serious health condition.

**Less favorable if you're healthy.** If you're in good health, a traditional fully underwritten policy will almost always be cheaper. Skipping the exam means the insurer can't verify your good health, so they charge more to account for the uncertainty.

## Who Benefits Most From No Exam Life Insurance?

### People With Busy Schedules

If coordinating a medical exam appointment feels like one more thing you don't have time for, accelerated underwriting removes that barrier entirely. You can apply during your lunch break and have a decision by dinner.

### People With Health Anxiety or Needle Phobia

It sounds minor, but needle phobia and medical anxiety prevent real people from getting coverage they need. No exam options remove that obstacle.

### People With Pre-Existing Conditions

If you have diabetes, a history of cancer, heart disease, or other chronic conditions, traditional underwriting might result in high rates or a decline. Simplified issue lets you answer targeted questions and potentially get coverage without your full medical history being scrutinized. Guaranteed issue accepts everyone.

### Older Adults Seeking Final Expense Coverage

Adults ages 60-85 who need a smaller policy to cover funeral costs, outstanding debts, or leave a small inheritance often find guaranteed issue to be the most practical option, especially if health has declined.

### Anyone Who Needs Coverage Immediately

Closing on a mortgage, finalizing a business loan, going through a divorce — life events that require proof of insurance don't always give you 6-8 weeks to complete traditional underwriting.

## Life Insurance No Medical Exam No Waiting Period

If your priority is **both** no exam and no waiting period, your options are:

1. **Accelerated underwriting term or whole life** — No exam, no waiting period, coverage up to $1M+. Requires good health.
2. **Simplified issue term or whole life** — No exam, no waiting period, coverage up to $500K. Requires answering health questions.

**Guaranteed issue is the only no exam product with a waiting period.** If avoiding both an exam and a waiting period is important, start with accelerated underwriting or simplified issue.

## How to Choose the Right No Exam Policy

### Step 1: Assess Your Health Honestly

If you're generally healthy with no major conditions, **accelerated underwriting** gives you the most coverage for the least premium increase. If you have manageable health conditions, try **simplified issue.** If you've been declined elsewhere, **guaranteed issue** is your safety net.

### Step 2: Determine Your Coverage Need

Calculate how much coverage your family needs. If the answer is $500,000+, accelerated underwriting is likely your only no exam option. For smaller amounts, simplified or guaranteed issue may work.

### Step 3: Compare Carriers

Not all no exam products are created equal. Premium, coverage limits, health questions, and waiting periods vary significantly between insurance companies. An independent agent can compare options across multiple carriers.

### Step 4: Read the Fine Print

Pay attention to the **graded death benefit** in guaranteed issue policies. Understand exactly what happens if you die during the waiting period. Look for exclusions, premium guarantees, and conversion options.

### Step 5: Consider Your Long-Term Plan

A no exam policy can serve as a **bridge.** Get coverage now, then apply for a fully underwritten policy later when you have time for the exam or your health improves. Once the new policy is approved, you can drop the more expensive no exam policy.

## The Application Process

Applying for no exam life insurance is straightforward:

1. **Choose your product type** based on your health, coverage needs, and budget.
2. **Complete the application** — online, by phone, or with an agent. You'll provide personal information, beneficiary details, and (for simplified issue and accelerated underwriting) answer health questions.
3. **Authorization** — For accelerated underwriting, you'll authorize the insurer to access electronic health records and databases.
4. **Decision** — Accelerated underwriting decisions come within hours. Simplified issue takes 1-3 days. Guaranteed issue is near-instant.
5. **Policy delivery** — Once approved, your policy is issued and coverage begins immediately (except for guaranteed issue's graded period).
